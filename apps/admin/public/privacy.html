Information stored locally

Spark stores the content needed to operate the app in an encrypted SQLite database on your device. Spark offers readable JSON export, password/recovery-code encrypted export, and an optional bounded encrypted backup to one Android folder you choose. Spark never receives those files or the recovery code. Readable JSON should be treated as private.

Deliberate sharing and calendar handoff

A progress image or text is created only from completed actions you explicitly select and is sent only through the system share sheet. Spark does not report progress automatically or connect an accountability account. Spark can also open the system create-event screen for one focus or leave-on-time block; it does not read or synchronize your calendars.

Device privacy controls

Optional app lock uses your device authentication. Spark does not receive biometric templates or your passcode. You can also protect the recent-app preview, prevent Android screenshots, and hide reminder details on the lock screen.

Optional cloud information

If you use support or purchases, Spark may process a random Firebase account identifier, the support text you submit, app version, platform, and Google Play purchase tokens required to verify entitlement. Spark administrators cannot view local habit or captured thought data.

Purpose, sharing, and retention

Optional cloud information is used to answer support, prevent purchase fraud, grant access, and operate the service. It is processed by Google Cloud/Firebase as a service provider and is not sold or used for advertising.

Anonymous cloud identities remain until the user deletes them or the operator publishes an inactive-account period. Local data remains until app storage is cleared or Spark is uninstalled. Exported backups remain wherever the user placed them.

Deletion and contact

Local data can be removed by clearing the app's storage or uninstalling it. Spark Settings can remove support conversations, the cloud entitlement, user record, and Firebase identity. Purchase, promotion, or security records that must be retained are disconnected from that identity with a random deletion pseudonym. Privacy requests can also be sent to REPLACE_ME@example.com.

Health disclaimer

Spark is an organization and self-management tool. It does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent ADHD or any medical condition, and it is not a substitute for professional care.

Children

Spark is not directed to children under the applicable age of digital consent. Do not enable cloud features for a child without reviewing local legal requirements.

Security

Native builds use encrypted local storage, operating-system credential storage, HTTPS for optional cloud requests, restricted administrator roles, and audited access grants. No storage or transmission method can guarantee absolute security, especially on a compromised or unlocked device.

Changes

Material policy changes will update the effective date and, where appropriate, be announced in Spark or its store listing.
